About Dr. Jennifer Parrott-Assistant Professor of English

Dr. Parrott joined the faculty at Clayton State in 2013. She teaches first-year composition, survey courses in American and British literature, and Writing for Digital Media. She teaches both online and seated courses, and she directs the English Department’s social media internship.

Prior to coming to Clayton State, Dr. Parrott worked in instructional technology at Bucknell University and taught first-year composition as a Marion L. Brittain Fellow at the Georgia Institute of Technology. She began her career in education as an Americorps volunteer in Chicago, where she worked as a literacy tutor.

She holds a Ph.D. in English from Southern Illinois University Carbondale, where she specialized in Modern British Literature and Irish Studies, and she has spent time studying and working in different parts of Ireland. Dr. Parrott earned her M.A. in English from DePaul University and her B.A. in English from Furman University.

Dr. Parrott’s research interests include Modern and contemporary Irish fiction and drama, composition pedagogy, and libraries as learning spaces in higher education.
