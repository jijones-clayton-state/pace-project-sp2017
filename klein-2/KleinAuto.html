About Dr. Sipai Klien

Experience

Assistant Professor, Clayton State University; Jan. 2012-Present

As an Assistant Professor, Dr. Klein developed and taught research-based, peer-education training course; taught technical communication, visual rhetoric, and introductory writing courses; participated in department assessment committee to streamline English majors' graduation requirements; participated in university committee on civic engagement; planned and implemented annual undergraduate and graduate civic engagement conference.

As the Studio's Director, Dr. Klein designed assessment instruments to evaluate student learning efficacy; wrote annual report on student engagement; participated in university accreditation process by reporting student engagement to university student support services; developed on-site and online writing support workshops; maintained annual university-wide, faculty-led writing workshops.

Instructional Technology Consultant, New Mexico State University; Jan. 2008-Jan. 2009

As an Instructional Technology Consultant, Dr. Klein conducted class- and web-based technology training to faculty, staff, and graduate assistants; designed multimedia instructional courses and training materials applying instructional design strategies; performed administration support of enterprise and university-wide learning technologies; reviewed, tested, and evaluated existing and new learning technologies well enough to see their usefulness and contribute to plans on how they could be integrated into the distributed learning technologies university-wide.

Online Instructional Coordinator, New Mexico State University; Aug.2005-May 2007

As an Online Instruction Coordinator, Dr. Klein designed and implemented the integration of online curriculum for the First-Year Composition Program; assisted First-Year Composition faculty and graduate teaching assistants in integrating learning management system and media into the classroom; designed assessment tools for online instruction.
